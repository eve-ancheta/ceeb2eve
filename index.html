Believe in Eve

Hi everyone, it's Eve.

In 2022, I came out as non-binary and chose the name "Ceeb"; it had been my online name since 2017. However, I didn't know myself. When I came out as Ceeb, I was isolated from the rest of the world: an unschooler, I'd never taken a class, I hadn't talked to other teens, and post-COVID, it'd been years since I engaged with anyone aside from my two younger siblings. I had zero idea that I was a girl. All I knew is that I wanted to be different, something other than a boy, even if it wasn't who I fully was. And so I chose "Ceeb."

"Ceeb" served its purpose. It gave me permission to exist within and then interact with the outside world as someone who wasn't a boy. That power allowed me to learn so, so much about myself: I'm an advocate, I'm a community builder, I'm a data science student. I'm a friend, a partner, and I'm apparently a Stanford superstar. I knew none of these things when I chose the name "Ceeb."

And so, I learned more about myself, "Ceeb" began to fit less and less. As early as 2024, I knew I needed a change, but it didn't come immediately. Quietly, I tried on different names — "CB," you might've seen — but none really stuck, and none truly spoke to the person I wanted to be and knew that I was: a feminine girl.

Following my Stanford acceptance, there was nothing to cloud me from the fact that a new life, a new era, was right ahead of me, and that I had the opportunity to step into it. I decided that I'd change my name…
