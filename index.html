From Ceeb to Eve

[This is a 2–3-minute read]



Hi everyone, it's Eve.

When I came out as “Ceeb” in 2022, I wasn’t ready to choose a new name.

I didn’t know myself. It was just after COVID, and I was isolated: as a deeply anxious unschooler, I'd never taken a class, and I hadn't talked to other teens. I had zero idea that I was a girl (I came out as non-binary) because I’d never even met one. All I knew is that I wanted to be different, something other than a disgusting boy, even if, deep inside, it wasn't who I really was. And so, I chose the familiar online name I’d gone by since 2017: “Ceeb.”

Through 4 years as “Ceeb,” I learned so, so much more about myself: I’m an advocate, I’m a community builder, I’m a friend, I’m a partner, and apparently, I’m a Stanford superstar. Most of all, I’m a feminine girl.

As I grew, “Ceeb” fit less and less. I started to resent it. Once, at a homeschool gathering, I introduced myself and a mom announced, “Everybody! Steve is here!” People think it’s “Ceb.” Speech-to-text thinks it’s “Sieb,” or worse, “Verb.” My name grew to give me feelings of both dysphoria and discomfort.

Once again, I decided I wanted to change my name. But this time, I was ready. This time, I knew who I was. I wanted to follow my happiness. I wanted a name that brought me joy, that reflected the person I knew I was — this time, I knew that.

I thought carefully about how I’d come to change it, seeing how hastily I’d arrived at “Ceeb.” Starting in 2024, I tried some names on — you might’ve seen “CB” or “Cecelia.” None of them felt right. I didn't want to give up, though.

It took 21 candidates and a rubric with 17 checkboxes. It took Namerology.com, Urban Dictionary, and article upon article about name upon name. It took weeknights up at 4 AM, but guess what? One and a half years later, I finally settled on a new name: “Eve.”

The feminine version of “Ceeb,” except it’s not a first-thought answer. A mathematical expression, e√e ≈ 4.48. Both people and digital transcription devices get it right. It fits that “Eve” means “new beginnings.” As “Ceeb,” I explored high school; now, as “Eve,” I’ll fly into my new beginning of College.

I couldn’t be happier with where I arrived. I’m proud of the process that led to Eve, but even more so, I’m proud of what “Eve” reflects — my growth. Now, I’ve talked to other teens. Now, I’ve taken classes and lived as my fullest self out in the world. Now, I don’t feel awkward sharing my name to people, and in fact it’s the opposite, because this time I really chose it. So now, as I’m “coming out” in 2026 — please, call me Eve! Unlike “Ceeb,” It’s who I am. <3

Thank you for reading,

Eve Ancheta (she/her)
